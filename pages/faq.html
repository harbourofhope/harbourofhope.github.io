Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first conversation.

Everything you need to know before your first conversation.

We understand that starting counselling can feel like a big step. Here are answers to some of the questions people commonly ask before beginning.

1 What is counselling?

Counselling is a safe and confidential conversation where you can openly share your thoughts, emotions and life challenges. It provides a supportive space to gain clarity, discover healthy ways of coping, and move towards healing and personal growth.

2 Are our conversations confidential?

Yes. Everything you share is treated with the utmost respect and confidentiality. Information remains private, except where disclosure is required by law or where there is a serious risk of harm to yourself or others.

3 How long is each counselling session?

A typical counselling session lasts approximately 50–60 minutes.

4 How many sessions will I need?

There is no fixed number of sessions. Every person's journey is different. Some people find a few sessions helpful, while others benefit from longer-term support. Together, we'll regularly review your progress and decide what best meets your needs.

5 How are counselling sessions conducted?

Harbour of Hope currently offers scheduled online counselling sessions through secure video conferencing, allowing you to connect from the comfort and privacy of your own space.

6 Is online counselling as effective as in-person counselling?

Yes. Research has shown that online counselling can be just as effective as in-person counselling for many emotional, relational and personal concerns. Many people also appreciate the convenience and comfort of receiving support from home.

7 How much does counselling cost?

Harbour of Hope is a not-for-profit counselling ministry. There are no fixed counselling fees. If you are able, you are welcome to make a voluntary donation to help cover the ministry's operating costs. The amount is entirely your decision.

8 How do I book an appointment?

Simply complete the Book Appointment form on our website. Esther will personally contact you to arrange a suitable day and time.

9 How can I prepare for my first session?

You don't need to prepare anything special. Simply come as you are. It may help to think about what you'd like to discuss or any questions you may have, but there is no expectation to have everything figured out before your first session.

10 What technology do I need for online counselling?

You'll need a reliable internet connection, a computer, tablet or smartphone with a camera and microphone, and a quiet, private place where you can speak comfortably without interruption.

11 How do I contact Esther if I have a question?

If you have a question before booking, you're welcome to:

  • Write to Esther
  • Chat on WhatsApp
  • Submit a Book Appointment request

Esther personally responds to every enquiry as soon as possible.

12 Is Harbour of Hope an emergency crisis service?

No. Harbour of Hope provides scheduled counselling by appointment and does not offer emergency crisis intervention.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or experiencing a mental health crisis, please contact your local emergency services or an appropriate crisis support service immediately.

Still have a question?

Esther personally responds to every enquiry — reach out any time.
